An electric color palette saturates Igor Moritz’s visceral depictions of loneliness and interpersonal turmoil. With oil paint or colored pencil, the Polish-born artist creates vivid, almost Fauvist backdrops and characters who stare with forlorn at the viewer. The resulting compositions appear to be equal parts Henri Matisse, Ernst Ludwig Kirchner, and Francis Bacon. ⁠
